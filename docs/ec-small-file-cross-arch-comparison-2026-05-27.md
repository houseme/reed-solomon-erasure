- x86_64 baseline: `benchmarks/small-file/2026-05-27-x86_64-linux-extended.csv` (`git_revision=9a1906e`, backend `rust-avx2`)

- `verify_with_buffer` is excluded from the cross-architecture table because it is absent from the archived AArch64 artifact.

## High-Level Readout

- `4+2 encode` splits evenly: x86_64 has lower latency for `4 KiB` through `256 KiB`, while aarch64 leads at `1 KiB`, `512 KiB`, and `1 MiB`.
- `4+2 verify` also splits evenly: x86_64 leads from `1 KiB` through `128 KiB`, while aarch64 leads from `256 KiB` upward.
- `4+2 reconstruct` and `4+2 reconstruct_data` favor x86_64 only at `1 KiB`, `4 KiB`, and `16 KiB`; aarch64 leads from `64 KiB` upward.
- `10+4 encode` and `10+4 verify` are also split 4 vs 4 across the measured sizes.
- `10+4 reconstruct` and `10+4 reconstruct_data` favor aarch64 on most medium and large sizes.
